CSS TYPE


MY REFLECTION

Doing Exercise 7 gave me a clearer understanding of how inline, internal, and external CSS each play a different role in styling a webpage. At first, switching between the three felt a bit confusing, but as I practiced, I began to appreciate how inline CSS is useful for quick, specific changes, while internal CSS keeps styles organized within a single file, and external CSS is the most efficient for larger projects. Experimenting with them helped me see how structure and design work together, and I realized how important it is to choose the right method depending on the situation.
