MY REFLECTION

In this activity, I learned how to create HTML forms and how to add links to a webpage. At first, I felt a little confused because forms have many parts, like text boxes, buttons, and labels. But as I practiced, I understood how each tag works and how important they are in collecting user information. I also learned how links help users move from one page to another, making a website easier to explore. During the activity, I enjoyed trying different input types, such as email, password, radio buttons, and checkboxes. It felt good to see the form come to life each time I refreshed the page. I also realized that even small mistakes in code, like missing quotation marks or tags, can affect the whole output, so I learned to double-check my work.
